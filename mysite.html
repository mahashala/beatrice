Research Portfolio

1. Tropospheric NO₂, Meteorology, and Seasonal Variability over Ghana Using TROPOMI

This study analyzed spatial and seasonal NO₂ concentration patterns across Ghana using TROPOMI satellite data. The research examined relationships between NO₂ hotspots and meteorological fields such as wind speed, boundary layer height, temperature, and convection. The project identified key seasonal contrasts between the dry and wet seasons and highlighted pollution signatures over Accra, Kumasi, and industrial zones.

2. SEIR Modeling of Tuberculosis (TB) in the Savannah Region of Ghana

This project incorporated meteorological variables into the classic SEIR epidemiological model to investigate how temperature, humidity, and rainfall influence TB incidence. The study performed temporal correlation analysis, trend assessment, and parameter sensitivity evaluation to understand climate–health dynamics.

3. SEIR Modeling of Typhoid Transmission

A mathematical modeling framework exploring disease transmission dynamics under varying environmental conditions. The model provided insight into exposure, infection cycle, and recovery processes as influenced by environmental stressors.

4. Greenhouse Gas Assessment over Farmland

A mini research project evaluating atmospheric greenhouse gas loading over agricultural environments. The study focused on anthropogenic versus natural emission contributors and their meteorological modulation.

5. Tropical Weather Systems Analysis

Developed a comprehensive set of 200-question MCQs from MET452 (Prediction of Tropical Weather Systems) to evaluate student understanding of West African monsoon dynamics, easterly waves, tropical cyclones, and convective weather processes.
