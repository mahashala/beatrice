About Me

I am Mahashala Michael Oppong, an Atmospheric Scientist and Climate Researcher with a strong interest in atmospheric composition, meteorology–pollution interactions, and climate-health linkages. I hold a degree in Meteorology and Climate Science from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST).

My academic and research work spans atmospheric NO₂ mapping, tropospheric dynamics, SEIR modeling for infectious diseases, tropical meteorology, environmental management, and the analysis of satellite-derived datasets such as TROPOMI. I employ Python, R, GIS, and quantitative analytics to interpret climate and environmental data and extract meaningful insights for policy, public health, and environmental decision-making.

I served as President of the Meteorology and Climate Science Students Association (MCSAK), where I led climate awareness campaigns, outreach to senior high schools in Kumasi, and capacity-building events for students. My portfolio also includes entrepreneurship (founder of Shala_Trades), science communication, digital media design, and youth empowerment content creation.

My long-term mission is to contribute innovative climate solutions through research, modeling, and scientific communication — bridging the gap between science, policy, and community impact.
